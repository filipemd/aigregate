AI’s Massive Water Footprint Collides with US Drought#

AI data centers consumed roughly 264 billion gallons of water in 2025, coinciding with drought conditions affecting 63% of the US. UN scientists warn that by 2030, AI’s water needs could match those of 1.3 billion people, while its electricity demand may triple that of 650 million people across Pakistan, Bangladesh, and Nigeria.

Bots Now Outnumber Humans on the Internet#

For the first time in history, machines generate more web traffic than people, with bots now accounting for 57.5% of all HTTP requests. This ‘platform shift’ threatens the economic foundation of the digital advertising and SaaS industries, which were built on the assumption of human attention.

  • https://www.forbes.com/sites/josipamajic/2026/06/04/bots-now-outnumber-humans-online-and-the-internet-was-never-built-for-this/

US States Pause Data Center Tax Credits Amid Resource Strain#

Illinois and Ohio have paused tax incentives for data centers as governors cite soaring electricity rates and water scarcity. The move follows significant community pushback and concerns that industrial AI demand is driving up utility bills for local residents.

  • https://fortune.com/2026/06/06/illinois-jb-pritzker-ohio-mike-dewine-pause-data-center-tax-credits-water-use-electricity/

Pokémon Go Data Allegedly Used for Military Drones#

Millions of Pokémon Go players may have unknowingly contributed to US military drone technology. Data from nearly 30 billion scans were reportedly used by Niantic Spatial to train 3D navigation systems for drones and robots that operate without GPS.

  • https://nltimes.nl/2026/06/06/scans-dutch-pokemon-go-players-may-helped-us-develop-military-drone-technology

Remote Hack Turns PC Speakers into Keyboards#

A vulnerability in Creative Sound Blaster Katana speakers allows attackers within 15 meters to remotely upload malicious firmware via Bluetooth. This transforms the speaker into a covert keyboard that can execute arbitrary commands on the connected PC without physical access.

  • https://blog.nns.ee/2026/06/03/katana-badusb/

ASML Becomes Europe’s Most Valuable Company#

ASML’s market cap hit $674 billion, surpassing Novo Nordisk to become the most valuable company in European history. The surge is driven by analyst projections that ASML can increase its output of critical EUV machines, directly expanding the global capacity for leading-edge AI silicon.

Claude AI Builds Entire Complex Software Repository#

A new browser-based Office Open XML viewer was implemented entirely by Anthropic’s Claude. The repository contains no human-written application code, utilizing Rust parsers compiled to WebAssembly and TypeScript renderers to display DOCX, XLSX, and PPTX files.
