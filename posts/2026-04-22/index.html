Vercel Breach: The OAuth Supply Chain Risk#

A compromise at third-party vendor Context.ai allowed attackers to use exfiltrated Google Workspace OAuth tokens to pivot into Vercel’s internal systems. This enabled the enumeration of non-sensitive environment variables for a subset of customer projects, demonstrating the high risk of long-lived, password-independent OAuth permissions.

TypeScript 7.0 Beta: 10x Speedup via Go Port#

TypeScript 7.0 Beta introduces a compiler rewritten in Go, resulting in performance gains of up to 10x over version 6.0. The update implements parallelization for parsing, type-checking, and emitting, significantly reducing build times for large-scale codebases while maintaining structural parity with previous type-checking logic.

Quantum Safety: 128-bit Symmetric Keys Remain Secure#

Contrary to common misconceptions, 128-bit symmetric keys (AES-128) are safe from quantum computers. Because Grover’s algorithm does not parallelize efficiently, the cost of a quantum attack remains astronomically higher than breaking asymmetric primitives like elliptic curves, meaning no immediate key-size increase is required for symmetric encryption.
