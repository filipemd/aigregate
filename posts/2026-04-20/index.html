EU Age-Verification App Hacked in Minutes#

Cybersecurity experts bypassed the European Union’s new age-verification app in under two minutes, discovering that sensitive data was stored unprotected on devices and biometric locks could be easily skipped. Despite the EU’s claims that the tool was ’technically ready,’ researchers found the code failed to meet basic security standards.

Vercel Breached via AI Tool OAuth Over-Permissioning#

A security breach at Vercel occurred after an employee granted a third-party AI tool, Context.ai, unrestricted access to their corporate Google Workspace. The attacker, linked to the ShinyHunters group, exploited this broad access to move laterally into internal systems and is now demanding $2 million for stolen environment variables.

Zoom Partners with World to Combat Deepfake Fraud#

To prevent multi-million dollar corporate frauds caused by AI-generated imposters, Zoom is integrating World’s ‘Deep Face’ technology. The system adds a ‘Verified Human’ badge to participants by cross-referencing their live video with a secure biometric registration, allowing hosts to require human verification in waiting rooms.

  • https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/17/zoom-teams-up-with-world-to-verify-humans-in-meeting/

The Shadow Economy of GitHub Fake Stars#

An investigation revealed a professionalized market where GitHub stars are sold for as little as $0.06 each to game VC sourcing algorithms. With some AI and blockchain repos showing up to 76% fake stargazers, the study highlights how inflated metrics are used to secure millions in seed funding despite a lack of genuine adoption.

Maryland Bans ‘Surveillance Pricing’#

Maryland has become the first US state to ban ‘surveillance pricing,’ prohibiting food retailers and delivery services from using a shopper’s personal data to tailor prices. The law aims to prevent companies from charging higher prices based on a user’s browsing history or location.

  • https://www.denver7.com/life/money/maryland-becomes-first-state-to-pass-bill-banning-surveillance-pricing

NASA’s ‘Big Bang’ Plan to Save Voyager Probes#

NASA is implementing a high-stakes energy-saving fix called ‘The Big Bang’ to extend the lives of the Voyager probes. After a power glitch on Voyager 1, engineers plan to replace groups of powered devices with lower-power alternatives to keep the spacecraft warm enough to continue gathering science data.

  • https://www.theregister.com/2026/04/20/voyager_big_bang_upgrades/

Palantir’s NHS Contract Faces Potential Termination#

The UK government is considering triggering a break clause to end Palantir’s £330 million NHS data platform contract. Critics and MPs argue the system is difficult to use and leaves the NHS with no ownership of the software built to connect various health trusts to the platform.
