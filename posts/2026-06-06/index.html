Bots Now Outnumber Humans on the Web#

For the first time in history, bot traffic has eclipsed human traffic online. This shift threatens the traditional ad-supported business model of the internet, as bots do not click ads, potentially forcing a transition toward charging AI services for access to human-generated content.

Anthropic Calls for Global AI Development Freeze#

Anthropic, now the world’s most valuable AI startup, is urging a global pause on frontier AI development. The company warns that the technology is approaching a tipping point where systems could self-evolve, creating an ‘arms control problem’ that could lead to catastrophic outcomes if not coordinated internationally.

Microsoft’s ‘Addictive’ AI Strategy Leaked#

Leaked internal documents reveal Microsoft intended to make its new AI assistant, Scout, ‘addictive’ to ensure daily user dependency. While CEO Satya Nadella publicly denied the strategy as ’nonsense,’ the documents were authored by the project’s own lead and senior executives.

US Government Eyes Equity Stakes in AI Giants#

The Trump administration is in preliminary talks with AI companies, including OpenAI, about the government acquiring equity stakes in these firms. The goal is to distribute AI-driven profits as dividends to American households, though critics warn this creates a conflict of interest where the regulator also becomes a shareholder.

  • https://www.notus.org/technology/trump-ai-stake-openai

AI CEOs Warn Congress of Bioweapon Risks#

Rivals Sam Altman (OpenAI), Dario Amodei (Anthropic), and Mustafa Suleyman (Microsoft) have co-signed a letter to Congress urging mandatory screening for the sale of synthetic biological materials. They warn that AI is rapidly eroding the knowledge barriers that previously prevented bad actors from creating bioweapons.

  • https://www.yahoo.com/news/politics/articles/ai-ceos-openai-anthropic-microsoft-081400108.html

Data Center Backlash Hits State Governments#

A growing wave of public and political resistance is stalling AI infrastructure. Illinois is suspending data center tax breaks, Virginia is seeing massive projects collapse due to local lawsuits, and Amazon engineers are publicly criticizing the company for spending $200 billion on data centers while laying off thousands of workers.

  • https://finance.yahoo.com/sectors/technology/articles/big-tech-desperate-amazon-engineers-081700769.html

FBI Warns of ‘Low-Tech’ In-Person Hacking#

The FBI has issued a warning that hackers are abandoning remote attacks in favor of posing as IT staff to gain physical access to corporate offices. This trend highlights a critical vulnerability: as AI makes digital defenses stronger, criminals are reverting to simple social engineering and physical intrusion to steal sensitive data.
