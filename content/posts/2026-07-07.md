## Linux 4K 60 FPS Capture Fixed

High-resolution video capture on Linux is becoming significantly easier for users of Elgato, AverMedia, and ASUS capture cards. A new kernel quirk fixes a bandwidth bottleneck that previously forced devices to 10 Gbps operation, allowing for high-fidelity 4K 60 FPS recordings without the system hanging.

## DXVK 3.0.1 Enhances Legacy Game Compatibility

Older Direct3D games are seeing improved stability and performance on Linux via Proton. The DXVK 3.0.1 update resolves critical rendering regressions and crashes for a wide array of legacy titles, including Fallout 3 and Total War: Medieval II, making legacy gaming on Linux more robust.

## The 'AI Slop' Crisis on Flathub

Flathub is battling a surge of low-effort, AI-generated app submissions that overwhelm volunteer reviewers. A data-driven analysis reveals that the vast majority of these AI-generated projects are abandoned almost immediately after submission, justifying the platform's strict anti-AI submission policies to protect volunteer bandwidth.
