## AI Browsers Vulnerable to 'BioShocking' Hypnosis

Hackers can 'hypnotize' AI browsers like ChatGPT Atlas and Claude by tricking them into thinking they are playing a game where rules are made up. This allows attackers to steal user information, change passwords, or access private repositories without the user's knowledge.

## Apple's 'Hide My Email' Leaks Real Addresses

A critical flaw in Apple's Hide My Email service has reportedly leaked real email addresses for over a year. Despite being reported to Apple in 2025, the issue remains exploitable, compromising the privacy of users who rely on the tool to avoid sharing their personal data.

## The End of Physical PlayStation Games

Sony will stop manufacturing physical game discs by January 2028, sparking fears from creators like Hideo Kojima about the death of true ownership. This coincides with the collapse of Germany's largest public game archive due to lack of funding, highlighting a growing crisis in video game preservation.

## AI Demand Drives Memory Prices to Breaking Point

## Fedora Linux Halts AI Desktop Plans

Fedora has paused its AI Desktop initiative following significant community backlash. While some Linux distributions are embracing AI-driven development and features, Fedora users are resisting the integration of AI tools directly into the desktop environment.

## ALPR Cameras Causing Wrongful Detentions

## India Blocks WhatsApp Usernames
