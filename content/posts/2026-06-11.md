## German Court Holds Google Liable for AI Hallucinations

A German regional court ruled that Google is directly liable for defamatory false claims made by its AI Overviews. The court rejected Google's defense that users should verify AI output, stating that because the AI rewrites content in its own words, it is the owner of that content and thus responsible for its accuracy.

## Disgruntled Researcher Drops Windows Defender Zero-Day

A bug hunter known as 'Nightmare Eclipse,' claiming to be a former Microsoft employee, has released a proof-of-concept exploit for a zero-day vulnerability called 'RoguePlanet.' The researcher is publicly disclosing flaws as revenge for alleged mistreatment and ignored reports by Microsoft.

## Meta AI Bot Facilitates 20,000 Account Hijacks

Over 20,000 Instagram accounts were stolen after hackers exploited a bug in Meta's AI-powered customer support bot. The flaw allowed attackers to request password reset links be sent to emails not associated with the target accounts.

## Starlink Shifts to Monthly Hardware Rental Model

Starlink has introduced a $10 monthly hardware rental fee for new residential customers in several countries, moving away from its traditional one-time purchase model. This shift mirrors the pricing strategies of traditional cable and telecom companies.

## Google Unveils DiffusionGemma for 4x Faster Text Generation

Google released DiffusionGemma, an experimental 26B MoE model that can generate text up to four times faster than standard LLMs on GPUs. By drafting entire paragraphs at once, it significantly reduces latency for local, interactive workflows.

- [https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/technology/developers-tools/diffusion-gemma-faster-text-generation/](https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/technology/developers-tools/diffusion-gemma-faster-text-generation/)

## Critical Privilege Escalation Bug Hits Arm CPUs

A critical security vulnerability affecting a wide range of Arm CPU cores, including the latest Cortex and Neoverse series, could allow attackers to escalate privileges. Linux kernel patches have been released to mitigate the flaw via additional TLB invalidation.

- [https://www.phoronix.com/news/Arm-CPU-Critical-CVE-2025-10263](https://www.phoronix.com/news/Arm-CPU-Critical-CVE-2025-10263)

## Claude Desktop Bug Spawns Massive Hidden VM

Windows users have reported that the Claude Desktop app consumes nearly 2 GB of RAM by spawning a Hyper-V virtual machine on every launch, even for simple chat use. The VM is triggered by stale session files from 'Cowork' mode and cannot be stopped without disabling the VirtualMachinePlatform.

- [https://github.com/anthropics/claude-code/issues/29045](https://github.com/anthropics/claude-code/issues/29045)
