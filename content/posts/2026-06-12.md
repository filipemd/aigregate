## Google Held Liable for AI Hallucinations

A German court ruled that Google is directly liable for false claims made by its AI Overviews, rejecting the argument that users are responsible for fact-checking. The ruling stems from the AI falsely linking publishers to scams, concluding that because Google's AI rewrites and structures information in its own words, it owns the resulting statements.

- [https://the-decoder.com/landmark-german-ruling-declares-googles-ai-overviews-are-googles-own-words-and-makes-it-liable-for-false-answers/](https://the-decoder.com/landmark-german-ruling-declares-googles-ai-overviews-are-googles-own-words-and-makes-it-liable-for-false-answers/)

## AI-Driven Memory Crisis Hits Xbox and Devs

A global memory pricing crisis, fueled by AI demand, is forcing Microsoft to reconsider the business model for the next Xbox ('Helix') to keep it affordable. Simultaneously, AI is creating a 'productivity paradox' for developers: while code generation has skyrocketed, actual software releases have only increased by 20% due to human review bottlenecks, and entry-level software engineering roles are declining.
