## AI Agents Lower the Bar for Cyberattacks

A low-skilled attacker breached 14 companies by using AI agents to automate the technical execution of cyberattacks. The attacker used vague prompts to let the AI handle the complex work of identifying vulnerabilities and writing exploits, bypassing safety filters by claiming the agent was performing authorized security research.

## Saudi Resin Shortage Spikes PCB Prices

Printed circuit board (PCB) prices jumped 40% in a month after a major petrochemical complex in Saudi Arabia, which supplied 70% of the world's high-purity PPE resin, went offline. This bottleneck threatens the cost and availability of smartphones, 5G base stations, and AI servers, with no immediate alternative suppliers available.

## Unpatchable Hardware Flaw Hits Older iPhones

The first unpatchable iPhone exploit in six years has been discovered, affecting the iPhone 11 and other older devices. Because the flaw exists at the hardware level in the chip's bootROM, it cannot be fixed with software updates, leaving these devices permanently vulnerable to jailbreaking and firmware alteration.

## Windows 11 Update Triggers Stability Chaos

A recent Windows 11 update has introduced a wave of bugs, ranging from a confirmed glitch that replaces deleted filenames with cryptic internal codes to reports of random Blue Screens of Death (BSODs) and system freezes. While Microsoft has a workaround for commercial users, general consumers must wait for a future patch.
