## Apple Patches FBI-Exploited Notification Bug

Apple released emergency updates for iOS and iPadOS after reports surfaced that the FBI recovered deleted Signal messages from a suspect's phone via internal notification storage. The bug allowed notifications to remain on the device even after the messages were deleted in-app or the app itself was removed.

## France ID Agency Breach Exposes 19M Citizens

The French government agency responsible for passports and national IDs confirmed a data breach affecting potentially 19 million records. A threat actor is currently offering the database for sale on hacking forums, significantly increasing the risk of targeted phishing and social engineering attacks against French citizens.

## UK Government Endorses Passkeys Over Passwords

The UK's National Cyber Security Centre (NCSC) has officially declared passkeys as the superior choice for authentication, stating they are more secure than even strong passwords paired with two-step verification. The agency now recommends passkeys as the first choice for all digital services.

- [https://www.techradar.com/pro/security/uk-security-agency-officially-declares-passkeys-superior-to-passwords-passkeys-should-be-the-first-choice-for-authentication](https://www.techradar.com/pro/security/uk-security-agency-officially-declares-passkeys-superior-to-passwords-passkeys-should-be-the-first-choice-for-authentication)

## Anthropic Admits to Claude Code Quality Dip

Anthropic detailed a series of engineering missteps that led to a perceived drop in Claude Code's intelligence. Issues included a bug that made the model 'forgetful' by clearing reasoning history every turn and a prompt change that limited verbosity but inadvertently hurt coding quality.

- [https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/april-23-postmortem](https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/april-23-postmortem)

## Raylib 6.0 Introduces GPU-Free Rendering

The massive raylib 6.0 update adds a software renderer, enabling graphics to run on CPU-only devices like the ESP32 microcontroller and upcoming RISC-V hardware. It also introduces new platform backends for Win32 and Emscripten to reduce external dependencies.

- [https://github.com/raysan5/raylib/releases/tag/6.0](https://github.com/raysan5/raylib/releases/tag/6.0)

## Git 2.54 Simplifies History Rewriting

Git 2.54 introduces an experimental 'git history' command to replace complex interactive rebases for simple tasks like fixing typos or splitting commits. It also modernizes hooks, allowing them to be defined in config files for easier sharing across repositories.

- [https://github.blog/open-source/git/highlights-from-git-2-54/](https://github.blog/open-source/git/highlights-from-git-2-54/)

## Ubuntu 26.04 LTS Launches with Outdated AI Tools

Ubuntu 26.04 LTS ('Resolute Raccoon') has launched, featuring a Rust-based sudo (sudo-rs) and Kernel 7.0. However, while it now allows easy installation of AMD's ROCm AI stack via apt, the provided packages are several months out of date, undermining the utility for AI developers.
